Respiratory Depression and/or Desaturation

  1. Call Rapid Response or Arrest immediately if clinically indicated.

  2. Get Help. Page 8-OUCH if necessary to alert pain service.

  3. Apply supplemental oxygen. Face masks are helpful and if no respiratory effort is made, assist the patient with a bag-valve-mask (often called an "Ambu bag"). Apply pulse oximeter.

  4. Consider giving naloxone. Titrating (0.04 mcg q5-10min) until adequate respiratory rate and depth observed. Beware possibilitiy of re-narcotization and naloxone may precipitate acute withdrawal in patients on chronic opioids.

  5. Discontinue all opioids and sedating medications; please inform prescribers of this so that alternatives may be ordered.
